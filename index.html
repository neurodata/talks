FAQ

Have a question that isn't addressed below? Email us at brainx@cis.jhu.edu

What is this meeting for?

The purpose of this workshop is to come together - as representatives of global neuroscience - to brainstorm and generate plans for how national, transnational and private brain programs can coordinate efforts to accomplish something synergistic and magnanimous across brain programs. Importantly, these plans will guide the content of an Autumn conference focused on facilitating international neuroscience collaborations, to be held at the UN during the General Assembly and hosted by the National Science Foundation and the Kavli Foundation.

What is the schedule

The conference will take place over 2 days, Thursday April 7 & 8, 2016. Thursday will begin with lightning rounds and followed by intensive group discussions, broken up by breaks and meals, and end with group presentations. Friday will consist of round robin group discussions to gather feedback and complementary perspectives, followed by final reporting. For details, see below.

Who will be in attendance?

Invitees span all continents, and range from academia, government, the private sector, and industry, with very diverse skills and interests. This includes representatives from all of the national, transnational, and private neuroscience initiatives, including the US BRAIN, the EU Human Brain Project, Japan Brain/MINDS, Korea Brain Project, China Brain Science Project, Brain Canada, Taiwan, and Australia, NSF, Kavli, Allen Institute for Brain Science, and HHMI, as well as Amazon, Google, DARPA, IARPA, and the White House. See below for details.

Where will it be held?

The conference will be held at Johns Hopkins University, hosted by the brand new Kavli Neuroscience Discovery Institute. Attendees from out of town will be staying across the street at the Inn at the Colonade.

This event has jointly been planned by Miyoung Chun & Chris Martin (Kavli), Jim Deshler (NSF), Joshua Vogelstein (Johns Hopkins), Rafael Yuste (Columbia), and Cori Bargmann (Rockefeller).
